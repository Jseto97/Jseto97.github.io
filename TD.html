TD Bank - My Forage into the World of Digital Strategy

Over the course of the summer of 2018 I was given the privilege and the opportunity to work at TD Canada Trust as a digital strategist on the Mobile Channels Innovation team. Our directive was to perpetuate the core values of TD, inclusion, user-centric design and uniqueness through innovative solution development.

As a digital strategist and pm intern I was exposed to the multi-faceted ecosystem of mobile development and design from the lens of digital strategy. Over the course of my internship I worked on two distinctive teams in mobile investing and mobile insurance. Defined by two simultaneous projects under and agile framework; my methodology over the internship was to take ownership of my work through the application of user empathy and design thinking. Over 4 months, 8 sprints and continuous iteration, my work lead to an eventual POC creation, user validation and patent filing for which I was listed as a primary inventor.

PWA Investigation

Under the guidance of my mentor and direct report Harj Panag, I conducted an investigation into the usage of Progressive Web Applications or PWAs, a framework released by Google in the 2017 developer summit; for which frontend developers could leverage to go beyond mere responsiveness in web applications. The investigation focused on the application of PWAâ€™s in the mobile banking space for the creation of prototypes that were scalable and responsive; allowing a depth of visualization and testing that a wireframe could not provide. My findings were summarzied in a short presentaion and infographic, leading to the usage of Progressive Web Applications in the development of Proof of Concepts within the mobile channel teams.

Chat Bot Competitor Analysis

One of my earliest projects as a PM was to conduct competitor analysis for the release of TD's Chatbot. This project would lead to the eventual creation of one of the defining projects of my internship. In order to visualize the data from my research I needed a way to display screens and user flows of multiple competitors in a cohesive environment. I decided to deviate from the norm of slide decks and utilized my knowledge of the design tool Figma to create an unorthodox break down of competitor onboarding. The result was a figma "mockup" that allowed the presenter to easily navigate through multiple different screens, with notable elements being highlighted by figma comments. My creative usage of a non-traditional methodology was presented to the wealth board of directors and commended, leading to the approval of 7 million dollars in funding for the development of a chat-based onboarding process.
